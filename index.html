The Venice of the North

Amsterdam is the capital of the Netherlands and the most populated city, reaching almost one million inhabitants. lies in the province of Noord-Holland. Because of the large amount of canals it has, it is usually referred to as the Venice of the North. The name Amsterdam comes from Amstelredamme which is a reference to the city’s origin as a small fishing village around a dam on the river Amstel in the 12th century.

One of the most culturally rich cities in all of Europe, Amsterdam is full of museums celebrating the works of great artists such as Rembrandt and Van Gogh, theatres and music halls. Festivals such as Koningsdag are celebrated by hundreds of thousands of people both living in and visiting the city. Tourists are usually also fascinated by the cycling infrastructure that Amsterdam offers, cycling is not a leisure activity (although it can be), it is the main form of transport in the city, more so than both public transport and cars, everyone from kids to elderly people travels through the special bike roads that connect the entirety of the city.

The melting pot.

Amsterdam is known for housing 180 different nationalities within its space, however there is one neighborhood that has the lion’s share. And that is Bos en lommer, while only 2.7 square kilometers large, 127 of these nationalities live in this small space. Dutch, Turkish, Moroccan and many other influences can be seen all over the neighborhood.

Shopping

When it comes to shopping various stores can be found in the Bos en lommerplein, ranging from big chain clothing shops such as C&A and HEMA, to smaller family owned ones the likes of Manzaram, and even the flea market occupying the square just outside the stores, where all kinds of commodities can be found. In an area so small you can find yourself spending hours shopping without even noticing.

In the underground area of the square you’ll find three supermarkets facing off against each other, trying to snatch you away from their competitors. That is of course if you have any money left from your visit up top.

Companies

Bos en lommer is also home to many company branches. From housing companies such as Rochdale to house-moving services such as MBI, multiple TAXI businesses, banks, hotels, space rental agencies, travel agencies, bookstores, and even printing services.

Touristic attractions

Despite all that was mentioned above, Bos en lommer still has more than just commercial attractions to offer. Erasmus park, one of the largest green areas in Amsterdam is a breath of fresh air both literally and figuratively, throughout the whole year it never runs out of visitors, during the summer it becomes an ideal picnic location where tourists and locals alike can enjoy the sun, barbecue,playing football and overall having a good time with their loved ones. Winter has its own unique activities, mainly ice gliding, snow fights and given the large amount of snow…making snowmen. During the rest of the year, people bring their pets to the park for a stroll, others come for a run, and some just enjoy a few moments of silence sitting on the bench. Podium mozaiek, an international theatre and Cafe-restaurant is another major attraction in Bos en lommer. It offers world music, theatre, exhibitions, and spoken word from national and international artists. You can also enjoy the relaxed atmosphere of the Café-restaurant before or after a show.

Sport.

If you are looking for a sports club, Amsterdam has got you covered, being a European capital, there is no shortage of clubs of all kinds of sports to satisfy your needs. We have compiled a list of 10 sport clubs that we think will grab your attention.

Amsterdam Sports Club By Strong Willpower (SDW)

The world’s favorite sport: Football. Whether for yourself or for your kid, this club offers training sessions, practice matches and even amateur competitions for all ages.

Blue White (korfball)

Centered around the Dutch sport Korfball, which is inspired by ringball a Swedish sport, males and females play together in teams of 8. This club offers lessons, matches and competitions for both kids and adults.

Flowmotion

Running is not as simple as you might have thought, with a group of enthusiasts you will learn new techniques to improve your running and will learn to get rid of any bad jogging habits.

Sportclub Match & Eetcafé Thijs (squash)

7 squash courts, lessons for adults and kids, and an online platform from where you will be matched with other players for a friendly competition.

Amsterdam Tennis Academy

With eight professional indoor tennis courts and a head coach with over 35 years of experience, this club offers tennis training programs for youth, adults, people who only have time in the morning, and to tourists just visiting the city.

Aikidojo Amstelveen

Learn the art of peace Aikido, created to allow unarmed men to defend against sword wielding opponents. This school offers beginner and advanced courses, also courses specialized for kids with special needs and those with weight problems.

Kung Fu School Golden Dragon Amsterdam West

Strengthen your body but also your mind and soul, thanks to the traditional way of teaching you will learn five Kung Fu styles in addition to weapon techniques, and only for the small price of 17 euro/mth.

Kickboxing Fit

Intense training in duo’s, designed to always make ascend to the next level, your physical condition and mental toughness will improve drastically. And if you are not a fan of the smelly halls, don’t worry, all training sessions take place OUTSIDE!!

Top Row

Explore the beautiful canals of Amsterdam while exercising, rowing is great sport that can be practiced throughout the whole year, the club offers team and private lessons for all levels.

Team leijdekker

Learn one of the most effective martial arts in the world today, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu. By practicing this art you will be able to keep up and even dominate opponents bigger than you. Sign up for a trial lesson and discover the genius behind it.

All you need now, is decide which one to join!
